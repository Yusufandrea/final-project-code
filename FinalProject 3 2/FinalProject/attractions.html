The Tower Of London

The Tower of London is a historic castle located in the heart of London. Built in the 11th century, it has served as a royal palace, a prison, and a place of execution. Today, it is home to the Crown Jewels and is a popular tourist attraction.

Price: £25 per adult

The British Museum

The British Museum is a world-famous museum located in the Bloomsbury area of London. It contains over 8 million works from all continents and covers over 2 million years of human history. Visitors can see artifacts such as the Rosetta Stone and the Parthenon sculptures. Admission to the museum is free, but donations are encouraged.

Price: £5 per person

The Grant Museum of Zoology

The Grant Museum of Zoology is a hidden gem located in the University College London. It's a small museum with over 68,000 zoological specimens covering the whole animal kingdom, from tiny insects to massive whales. Visitors can see a variety of fascinating exhibits, including a dodo skeleton, a quagga skeleton, and a jar of moles. Admission to the museum is free, but donations are encouraged.

Price: £5 per person

The National Gallery

The National Gallery is an art museum located in Trafalgar Square. It houses a collection of over 2,300 paintings dating from the mid-13th century to 1900, including works by Botticelli, Leonardo da Vinci, Rembrandt, Van Gogh, and many others. Visitors can explore the galleries and admire the masterpieces on display. Admission to the museum is free, but donations are encouraged.

Price: £5 per person
