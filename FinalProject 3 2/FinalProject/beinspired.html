Be Inspired - Top Rated Attractions in London

Covent Garden Market

Covent Garden Market is a historic covered market in the heart of London's West End. It's known for its unique shops, street performers, and restaurants. Visitors can find everything from handcrafted jewelry to gourmet food.

Rating: 4.8/5

Visit Official Website

London Eye

The London Eye is a giant Ferris wheel located on the South Bank of the River Thames. It offers stunning views of the city from a height of 135 meters.

Rating: 4.6/5

Visit Official Website

British Museum

The British Museum is a world-renowned museum that showcases the history and culture of human civilization from its earliest days to the present. Its collection includes over 8 million objects and it's free to visit.

Rating: 4.7/5

Visit Official Website

Hyde Park

Hyde Park is one of the largest parks in London and is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. It offers a variety of activities such as boating, cycling, and horse riding. It's also home to famous landmarks such as the Serpentine Lake and Speakers' Corner.

Rating: 4.8/5

Visit Official Website

National Gallery

The National Gallery is an art museum that houses one of the greatest collections of paintings in the world, dating from the 13th century to the 1900s. It's located in Trafalgar Square and is free to enter.

Rating: 4.8/5

Visit Official Website

Tower Bridge

Tower Bridge is an iconic landmark in London that spans the River Thames. Visitors can walk across the bridge's high-level walkways and enjoy panoramic views of the city. It's also home to the Tower Bridge Exhibition, which tells the story of the bridge's construction and history.

Rating: 4.5/5

Visit Official Website
